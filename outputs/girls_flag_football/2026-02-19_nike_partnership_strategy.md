## Negotiation Playbook

### What Nike Will Push Back On
1. **Cash:** They will want to do product-only first. Accept this for Year 1. Write Year 2 cash escalation into the contract.
2. **Exclusivity ask:** They will want it for free. Require minimum product threshold to grant exclusivity.
3. **Content rights scope:** They will want broad rights. Protect: player names/likenesses, school district rights, any underage player content must require individual parent release.
4. **Term length:** They will want 1 year with no commitments. Push for 1 year + 2-year option with escalation triggers.

### Power Moves Before the Meeting
- **Have Under Armour talking:** Even exploratory emails with UA create genuine urgency. You don't need a UA deal — you need Nike to know they could lose exclusivity.
- **Show the Cardinals letter:** Any formal engagement letter from Arizona Cardinals elevates your legitimacy instantly. Nike respects NFL relationships.
- **Bring the highlight reel:** Open the meeting with 60 seconds of your best game footage. No slides until they've seen the girls play.
- **Lead with the national vision:** Nike does not sign AZ-only programs. They sign programs that will be national in 3 years. Open with the expansion map.
- **Know their comps:** Nike sponsors NFL Flag nationally. Your program is their girls channel. Position as complementary, not competitive.

### Non-Negotiables (Do Not Give Away)
- Do not grant exclusivity without minimum product value threshold ($25K minimum)
- Do not allow Nike to use images of individual underage players without signed parent release forms included in the contract
- Do not sign a deal that limits your ability to accept grants from non-apparel sponsors (Nike should only restrict apparel/footwear category)
- Always retain right to end relationship if Nike's brand becomes controversy-adjacent
