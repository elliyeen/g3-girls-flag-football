## Phase 3: Nike Partnership Pursuit
**Duration:** June – November 2026 (concurrent with Phase 2/4)
**Goal:** Signed Nike sponsorship agreement — Year 1 deal

### The Nike Approach Strategy

Nike does not respond well to cold outreach from unknown organizations. The path to a Nike deal is through proof, warm introductions, and alignment with Nike's existing programs.

**Three Entry Points — Pursue all simultaneously:**

**Entry Point A: NFL Flag → Nike**
NFL Flag is the NFL's official youth flag football program. Nike is the NFL's official uniform supplier. This is the cleanest warm path. Secure an NFL Flag girls chapter affiliation → request NFL Flag to introduce you to their Nike contacts.

**Entry Point B: Nike Community Impact Fund**
Nike has a formal community grant program. Applications open periodically. A girls' flag football program with 100+ players, school district partnerships, and a Title IX alignment story is a strong candidate. Grant range: $10,000–$100,000.

**Entry Point C: Nike AZ Territory Manager**
Nike assigns regional territory managers for community and grassroots sports partnerships. The AZ territory manager handles smaller-scale local deals. This is faster than HQ but requires a warm intro. Path: through AZ Cardinals community team or local Nike retailer relationships.

### Nike Pitch Deck — Required Sections
1. **The Problem** — Girls are underrepresented in organized football. 40% of youth sports participants are girls; less than 8% play football variants.
2. **The Mission** — A girls-first flag football organization that builds confidence, athletic competence, and community.
3. **The Traction** — X players registered, Y school districts, Z media mentions, A social following.
4. **The Brand Story** — Show the brand. Show the girls. Show the moments. Nike buys emotion and culture.
5. **The National Vision** — AZ is the proof of concept. Next: TX, CA, FL. Total addressable market in 3 years: 10,000+ girls.
6. **The Ask** — See sponsorship structure below.
7. **The ROI for Nike** — Association with fastest-growing girls' sport, Title IX alignment, NFL co-brand halo, social content rights.

### Nike Sponsorship Ask — Year 1

**Tier 1 Ask (Minimum Viable Deal):**
- $25,000–$50,000 in Nike product (uniforms, cleats, training gear, balls)
- Nike logo on all uniforms and website
- Co-marketing rights for Nike to use content from our events
- Annual review with option to upgrade

**Tier 2 Ask (Preferred Deal):**
- $50,000 Nike product + $25,000 cash
- Naming rights to Season championship event
- Nike athlete ambassador (1 local AZ female athlete) for one season
- Social media co-posts (Nike Girls in Sport channel)

**What Nike Gets:**
- 100–250 girls wearing Nike gear in AZ (visible, photographable, shareable)
- Content rights for Nike social/marketing use
- Community impact story aligned with Nike's "Girls in Sport" and "Sport Changes Everything" campaigns
- First mover position in the fastest-growing girls' sport
- National expansion commitment — deal grows with the program

### Negotiation Guidance
- **Never lead with cash ask.** Lead with product. Product is low-cost for Nike and high-value for you.
- **Proof beats pitch.** Walk in with a full season impact report, player photos/video, and media clips. Nike's team needs to bring something back to their team.
- **Exclusivity is your leverage.** You have not yet signed Under Armour or Adidas. Offer category exclusivity (footwear, apparel) in exchange for a better deal.
- **Tie to NFL Flag.** If you have NFL Flag affiliation, Nike is already on the table as a supplier. Make the connection explicit.
- **Build FOMO.** If you have even a conversation with Under Armour, mention it (not threateningly — just factually). Nike responds to competitive urgency.
- **Start local, scale terms.** Year 1 deal with year 2/3 escalation terms written in (e.g., if national registration reaches 1,000, product allocation doubles).
