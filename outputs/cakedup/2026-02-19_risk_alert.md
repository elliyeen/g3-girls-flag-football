## 🔴 RISK-001 — Pricing Too Low to Reach Target (Score 9 — Critical)
**Category:** `budget` | **Owner:** AT | **Signal:** `BLOCKER`

**The problem:** At $3/cake, reaching $350/month requires selling ~117 cakes per month — about 4 cakes every single day. That is an unsustainable workload for one person.

**The math:**
```
Current: $50/month = ~16 cakes
Target:  $350/month
  → At $3/cake only:  117 cakes needed (+101 more per month)
  → With custom tier: 40 regular + 8 custom at $20 = ~$360/month ✅
```

**Resolution — MS-009 (Custom Cake Tier, due Apr 7):**
Introduce decorated custom cakes starting at $15. Two custom orders per week generates $160/month on its own. This single change makes the revenue target achievable.

**This is a blocker on MS-010 (Revenue $350/month).** The target cannot be reached on regular pricing alone without unsustainable volume.

## 🟡 RISK-002 — Cake Photos Not Ready (Score 6 — High)
**Category:** `schedule` | **Owner:** AT

No cake photos = no website launch. No website launch = no Phase 2 marketing.

**What you need before Mar 4:**
- 5 photos minimum
- Natural light (by a window) is all you need — no camera required, phone works great
- Take photos before delivering — once a cake is gone, that chance is gone
- Shoot from above (flat lay) and from the side
- Use a clean background — white plate, plain table, or a piece of cardboard covered in kraft paper

**Quick tip:** Canva free templates can make a basic phone photo look professional with a simple frame and your logo added on top. Your photos don't need to be perfect — they need to be real and appetizing.

## 🟡 RISK-003 — Social Media Consistency (Score 6 — High)
**Category:** `schedule` | **Owner:** AT

The most common reason small food businesses stall is launching social media and then stopping. Inconsistent posting tells the algorithm you're not active — and it stops showing your content to new people.

**The minimum viable schedule:**
- 3 posts per week on Instagram
- 1 TikTok video per week

**How to make this sustainable:**
Every Sunday, spend 20 minutes preparing next week's 3 Instagram posts in Canva. Save them to your phone. Post them Monday, Wednesday, Friday. This takes 10 seconds each posting day when the content is already made.

**Warning signs:** If you miss a full week, make a "we're back" post that same day. Don't let it become 2 weeks.
