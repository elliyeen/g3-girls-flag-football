## The Math — Adjusted for Personal Cakes

AT sells personal-sized cakes to students. Not large decorated cakes. The revenue model needs to reflect that.

| Strategy | Orders needed | How | Realistic? |
|---|---|---|---|
| $3 singles only | 117/month | ~4 cakes every school day | Tough |
| $5 pairs only | 70 orders/month | ~3 pair-orders every school day | Doable if more students know |
| Mix + bundle deals | ~60 regular + 10 bundles | More per transaction | ✅ Better |
| Mix + occasion packs | ~50 regular + 5 packs at $12 | Birthday gift packs | ✅ Target met |

**The key insight:** AT doesn't need to sell 117 individual cakes. She needs to sell to more students AND get each student to spend a little more per order. That comes from bundles and occasion packs — not large decorated cakes.

---

## Revised Pricing Strategy

Keep the core pricing. Add three new options:

| Item | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 personal cake | $3 | Keep as-is |
| 2 personal cakes | $5 | Keep as-is |
| Bundle — 3 cakes | $7 | $0.33 savings — easy upsell |
| Bundle — 5 cakes | $10 | Good for sharing with friend group |
| Birthday Pack | $12–$15 | 5–6 cakes packaged as a gift, small note included |

**The Birthday Pack is the high-value tier.** It's not a decorated cake — it's the same personal cakes, but packaged as a gift for a friend's birthday. Tied with a ribbon or in a box. Students give these to friends. One pack = $12–$15 vs. $3 for a single cake.

## Phase 2 — Launch & Announce (Mar 4 → Mar 31) · Target: $100

### At School — This is the #1 Channel

Word of mouth at school is faster and more powerful than any social media for a student market. Start here.

**Tell 10 people personally on launch day.**
Not a broadcast, not a group chat. Tell 10 individual classmates directly:
> *"Hey I have a website now — you can pre-order cakes from me. $3 each, $5 for two. Order the day before and I'll bring them in."*

Those 10 people tell 3 people each. That's 30 new potential customers in one week.

**The pre-order announcement:**
> *"Cake'd Up now takes pre-orders! Order by [day] night, pick up [next day] at school. Link in my bio 🎂"*

Post this on Instagram and send it as a DM to every past customer.

**The lunch-spot advantage:**
If AT can sell at lunch or between classes, presence = sales. Students who see the cakes want one. Visibility at the point of sale is marketing.

Young entrepreneur content is one of TikTok's most-shared categories. AT has a built-in story that adults with businesses don't have.

## Phase 3 — Bundle + Occasion Packs (Apr 1 → Apr 30) · Target: $350

### Introduce Bundle Pricing (Apr 7)

Add to the website, order form, and social media bio:

**3 cakes — $7** · **5 cakes — $10** · **Birthday Pack (5–6 cakes + note) — $12–$15**

Post about it:
> *"New! Birthday Packs are here 🎉 5 of my personal cakes packaged up as a gift for your friend — $12. Link in bio to order 🎂🩷"*

A Birthday Pack post showing the packaging will perform very well. Students buy these for friends constantly — they just didn't know they could order from AT before.

### The Birthday Pack

This is the single most powerful revenue lever for the student market. Here's how to make it:
- 5–6 personal cakes (same as always)
- Placed in a small box or tied in a clear bag with ribbon (dollar store supplies, < $1 per pack)
- Small handwritten note or printed card: *"From [sender] — Cake'd Up 🎂"*
- Price: $12–$15 depending on packaging

One Birthday Pack per week = $52–$60/month on top of regular sales.

### Seasonal Specials

Small seasonal twist on the personal cakes — same cake, small themed decoration or packaging:

| Occasion | When | Idea | Price |
|---|---|---|---|
| Valentine's Day | Feb 14 | Pink or red icing swirl, heart sprinkles | $4 each |
| St. Patrick's Day | Mar 17 | Green icing | $4 each |
| Spring / Easter | Apr | Pastel sprinkles | $4 each |
| Back to school | Sep | School colors | $4 each |

A $4 seasonal cake costs AT almost nothing extra in ingredients. The small premium is for the theme. Post a teaser photo 1 week before: *"Valentine's Day specials dropping soon 💕 — pre-order opens Monday."*

### Phase 3 Marketing Actions

**1. "Slots left this week" every Monday**
Post a Story: *"This week: [X] slots left. Pre-order by Wednesday night. Link in bio."*
Scarcity drives orders. Students don't want to miss out.

**2. Referral deal**
> *"Get a friend to order and your next cake is free."*
Costs AT ~$0.50 in ingredients. Brings in a new recurring customer.

**3. Friend group targeting**
When a student orders a 5-pack, ask (in the order form notes): *"Is this for a group? Tag your friends!"*
Friend groups buy together. One order becomes five followers.

**4. Post every Birthday Pack you deliver**
With permission, post a photo of the pack before delivery. Tag the occasion (no need to tag the person). *"Birthday Pack going out today 🎉🎂"* — these posts make other students want to order one for their friend.

## What NOT to Do

| Don't | Why |
|---|---|
| Try to sell large decorated cakes | That's a different business and a different market. AT's edge is personal cakes for students. |
| Pay for Instagram ads | Organic growth and school word-of-mouth is faster for this market. |
| Post inconsistently | Batch content every Sunday. 3 posts/week is the minimum. |
| Undersell the Birthday Pack | $12–$15 is fair. Students pay more for convenience and presentation. |
| Skip the pre-order system | Baking without pre-orders = waste + stress. Orders first, bake second. |
| Ignore DMs | Every unanswered DM is a lost sale. Reply same day. |
