## Lessons Learned — Full Detail

### LL-MED-001 · `resource` · HIGH
**Pattern:** `key_role_gap_at_project_start`
**Observation:** IT Lead role undesignated at kickoff; 4 milestones assigned to 'TBD'. Role gap persisted into Phase 1 execution and now blocks the critical path.
**Action:** Require all workstream lead roles designated and accepted before project kickoff gate.
**Applicable to:** `technical`, `mixed` | `medium`, `enterprise`

---

### LL-MED-002 · `compliance` · MEDIUM
**Pattern:** `regulatory_dependency_on_critical_path`
**Observation:** Permit timeline not confirmed in Phase 1. Permit application is on the critical path to Phase 2 contractor start. No buffer was built in at planning.
**Action:** In any project requiring regulatory approval, confirm the regulatory agency's processing timeline in Phase 1 before issuing Phase 2 contracts.
**Applicable to:** all project types | `medium`, `enterprise`

---

### LL-MED-003 · `compliance` · MEDIUM
**Pattern:** `legal_instrument_not_started_before_vendor_work`
**Observation:** Lease amendment drafting has not started as of Feb 19; contractor work cannot start until it is executed (Mar 15). Tracking this only in risk mitigation notes caused it to be deprioritized.
**Action:** Any legal instrument that must precede vendor work should be tracked as a project milestone with a named legal team owner — not left in risk notes.
**Applicable to:** `mixed`, `non_technical` | `medium`, `enterprise`

---

### LL-MED-004 · `resource` · HIGH
**Pattern:** `key_role_gap_at_project_start`
**Observation:** RISK-008 (network downtime) is owned by 'IT Lead (TBD)'. No one is actually tracking or mitigating it.
**Action:** All risks must have a named human owner. 'TBD' is not a valid risk owner. Risk register should be reviewed at kickoff.
**Applicable to:** `technical`, `mixed` | all scales

### LL-MED-005 · `compliance` · MEDIUM
**Pattern:** `regulatory_dependency_on_critical_path`
**Observation:** Permit application deadline (Feb 28) and RFP issuance (Feb 27) fall in the same sprint, compressing two deadline-sensitive actions into one week.
**Action:** Sequence permit application before RFP when they share a sprint. Permit has external dependency (regulatory agency) and less control.
**Applicable to:** `mixed`, `technical` | `medium`, `enterprise`

### LL-MED-006 · `vendor` · MEDIUM
**Pattern:** `vendor_dependency_on_internal_deliverable`
**Observation:** NetRun cannot confirm VLAN compatibility until they receive the IT requirements document (MS-003). An internal milestone is the input to a vendor action — a hidden dependency.
**Action:** When a vendor deliverable depends on an internal milestone, make the dependency explicit in both TOML records and track in sprint planning.
**Applicable to:** `technical`, `mixed` | `medium`, `enterprise`

## Pattern Library — This Run

Patterns confirmed or proposed by this project instance.

### pattern.resource.workstream_lead_gap
**Name:** Workstream Lead Gap
**Description:** A key workstream lead role is undesignated at project start. All milestones owned by that role become implicit blockers.
**Signal:** `blocker`
**Risk categories:** `resource`
**Milestone categories:** `it_infrastructure`, `technical`
**Mitigation:** Treat workstream lead designation as a Phase 0 / kickoff gate criterion.
**Seen in:** LL-MED-001, LL-MED-004, LL-MED-010, LL-MED-012

---

### pattern.resource.tbd_risk_owner
**Name:** TBD Risk Owner
**Description:** A risk register entry lists 'TBD' or a placeholder as owner. Risk is nominally tracked but has no accountable person.
**Signal:** `blocker`
**Mitigation:** Risk ownership assignment is a kickoff gate. No risk may be opened without a named, confirmed owner.
**Seen in:** LL-MED-004

---

### pattern.compliance.permit_on_critical_path
**Name:** Regulatory Dependency on Critical Path
**Description:** A regulatory approval sits on the critical path between planning and execution. Delay in approval propagates forward through all downstream milestones.
**Signal:** `dependency_done`
**Risk categories:** `compliance`, `schedule`
**Mitigation:** Confirm regulatory timeline in Phase 1. Build a buffer phase or parallel-track permit with RFP preparation.
**Seen in:** LL-MED-002, LL-MED-005

---

### pattern.compliance.legal_instrument_timing
**Name:** Legal Instrument Before Vendor Work
**Description:** A legal instrument (lease, contract, DPA) must be executed before vendor work can begin, but its drafting is tracked only in risk notes — not as a project milestone.
**Signal:** `blocker`
**Risk categories:** `compliance`
**Mitigation:** Treat every required legal instrument as a project milestone with a named legal owner and a hard start date.
**Seen in:** LL-MED-003

---

### pattern.vendor.internal_input_dependency
**Name:** Vendor Input Dependency
**Description:** A vendor action depends on an internal project milestone as its input. Often undocumented; surfaces as a surprise vendor delay.
**Signal:** `dependency_done`
**Mitigation:** At vendor onboarding, map all internal milestones that are inputs to vendor deliverables.
**Seen in:** LL-MED-006

---

### pattern.vendor.rfp_deadline_compression
**Name:** RFP Response Deadline Compression
**Description:** Multiple vendors issued RFPs with the same or near-same response deadline. Complex vendors (GC, IT) require more response time than simple vendors.
**Signal:** none
**Mitigation:** Stagger RFP response deadlines by vendor complexity. GC bids need 2–3 weeks minimum.
**Seen in:** LL-MED-007

---

### pattern.budget.baseline_approval_timing
**Name:** Budget Baseline Sequencing
**Description:** The formal budget baseline must precede the first vendor commitment. If baseline approval is concurrent with RFP issuance, bids arrive without an approved ceiling.
**Signal:** `dependency_done`
**Mitigation:** Budget baseline is a hard prerequisite (dependency) for RFP issuance.
**Seen in:** LL-MED-008, LL-MED-009

---

### pattern.change_management.parallel_start
**Name:** Change Management Parallel Start
**Description:** Change management activities must begin at project kickoff — in parallel with planning. Late start is one of the most common causes of adoption failure.
**Signal:** none
**Risk categories:** `org`
**Mitigation:** Include change management kickoff as a Phase 1 milestone with a named OCM/HR owner from Day 1.
**Seen in:** LL-MED-011
