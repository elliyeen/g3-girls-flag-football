# Welcome Email Template

*Instructions: Fill in the bracketed fields before sending. Send from the new hire's manager or from Jordan Ellis in People Operations, depending on your team's preference. Send the morning of the new hire's first day, or the evening before.*

---

**To:** [NEW HIRE EMAIL]
**From:** [MANAGER NAME] or Jordan Ellis \<jellis@client.com\>
**Subject:** Welcome to the team, [FIRST NAME] — everything you need for day one

---

Hi [First Name],

Welcome — we're really glad to have you here.

A few things to make your first day as smooth as possible:

**Your laptop** will be ready for you at [LOCATION — e.g., "the front desk" / "IT on the 3rd floor, Room 310"]. Bring your ID for badge pickup at the same time.

**Your first meeting** is with Jordan Ellis (People Operations) at [TIME] in [LOCATION / VIDEO LINK]. She'll walk you through the HR paperwork and answer any early questions.

**After that**, [MANAGER NAME] will meet you at [LOCATION] at [TIME] for a quick office orientation and team introductions.

Attached to this email:
- Your Welcome Document — one page, everything you need to know on day one
- Your Onboarding Checklist — your roadmap for the first two weeks

If anything comes up before you arrive, you can reach me at [MANAGER EMAIL / PHONE] or Jordan at jellis@client.com.

See you [DAY] morning.

[MANAGER NAME / JORDAN ELLIS]
[TITLE]
[PHONE — optional]

---

*Template notes:*
- *Keep the email short. The welcome document has the detail; this email just sets the logistics.*
- *Do not attach the checklist as a PDF — use the markdown or a shared doc link so it stays editable.*
- *Send a test to yourself before sending to the new hire to confirm attachments open correctly.*
- *For remote new hires: replace all location references with video links and confirm IT shipped the laptop at least 2 days before start date.*
