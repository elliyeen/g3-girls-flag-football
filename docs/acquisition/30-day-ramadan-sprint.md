## Week 3 — Days 15–21
### Demo calls. Handle objections. Build the data room.

### Demo Call Structure (every call)
1. **2 min** — ask about their current state, their problems
2. **3 min** — show the 3-minute demo video
3. **5 min** — live demo: `forge run-all` producing output in real time
4. **10 min** — their questions
5. **Close** — *"Does this fit what your team is looking for? Who else should be in the next conversation?"*

The goal of every call is the **next call with a more senior person.**

### Objections and Answers

| Objection | Answer |
|---|---|
| "We build our own" | "You build the models. We built the domain expertise, the regulated industry compliance layer, and the enterprise delivery pipeline. That took 2 years. Buying is faster than building." |
| "Too early stage" | "The platform runs in production today. HIPAA audit trail is live. PHI detection is live. SOC 2 started. We have [customer]." |
| "Not enough revenue" | "We are pre-scale by design. This is an acqui-hire of the team and the IP at the right moment — before the price goes up." |
| "We already have project management tools" | "Existing tools require humans to manage them. Forge has no humans in the loop. It runs, decides, acts, and reports — autonomously." |

## Week 4 — Days 22–30
### Push to LOI. Do not slow down.

### What a Serious Acquirer Will Ask For
- A deeper technical review — their engineers will look at the codebase
- A commercial discussion — price, structure, founder terms
- A reference call — they will speak to your customer
- Legal NDA — have it signed within 24 hours of request

### LOI Negotiation
An LOI covers:
- Acquisition price or range
- Structure — asset purchase vs stock purchase (stock is usually better for founders)
- Exclusivity period — typically 30–60 days for full due diligence
- Key founder terms — retention package, earnout, vesting acceleration
- Binding vs non-binding

**Do not negotiate an LOI without an M&A lawyer. Hire one before Day 20.**
Cost: $5,000–$15,000 to get to a signed LOI.
