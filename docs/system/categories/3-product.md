# 3 — PRODUCT

| | |
|--|--|
| **Category** | 3 |
| **Name** | PRODUCT |
| **Business Label** | PRODUCT |
| **Personal** | your craft — what you make, build, and offer |
| **Business** | What the organization sells, builds, or delivers to its customers — the thing that creates value and generates revenue |

---

## Business Sub-categories

| Sub-category | Description |
|---|---|
| Product Strategy & Roadmap | What gets built, in what order, and why — the plan for the product over time |
| Product Design & Development | Building the product — engineering, design, iteration, prototyping |
| Service Design | Designing service-based offerings — how they are structured, delivered, and experienced |
| User Experience (UX) | How customers interact with and experience the product or service |
| Features & Requirements | What the product does — documented, prioritized, and validated |
| Product-Market Fit | Validating that the product solves a real problem for real customers |
| Product Testing & QA | Ensuring what is built works correctly before it reaches customers |
| Product Launch & Go-to-Market | How the product reaches the customer — launch planning, positioning, rollout |
| Customer Feedback & Iteration | Listening to customers and improving the product based on what they say |
| Product Lifecycle Management | Managing the product from conception through retirement |
| Pricing & Packaging | How the product is priced, bundled, and presented for sale |
| Product Documentation | Guides, manuals, help content — everything that helps customers use the product |

---

## Fits Across Org Types

| Org Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Sole Trader | The service you offer — your craft, your expertise, your deliverable |
| Small Business | Core product or service line — what customers actually buy |
| Startup | The product being built — MVP, iteration, product-market fit search |
| SME | Formal product management, growing product line, service catalogue |
| Enterprise | Product portfolio management, global product teams, platform strategy |
| Government | Public services delivered — permits, benefits, information systems |
| University | Degree programs, courses, research outputs, online learning products |
| K-12 School | Curriculum, learning programs, extracurricular offerings |
| Nonprofit / NGO | Programs and services delivered to beneficiaries |
| Healthcare | Clinical services, treatment protocols, patient care products |
| Sports Team | The game experience, media content, merchandise, events |

---

## Orchestrator Routing

Requests classified as **3 PRODUCT** route to:
- New product or service → BA Agent (requirements) → Architect Agent (design) → UX Agent
- Product improvement → BA Agent → UX Agent → Build agents
- Pricing or packaging → BA Agent + Finance Agent (6 FINANCE)
- Product launch → BA Agent + 2 CUSTOMERS + 7 OPERATIONS
- Testing and QA → QA Agent
