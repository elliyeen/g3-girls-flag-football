## 1. Organization Structure

### Recommendation: File as 501(c)(3) Nonprofit
**Why:** A 501(c)(3) nonprofit (not an LLC) is the correct structure for this mission.

Benefits:
- Parent donations are tax-deductible (drives individual giving)
- Eligible for Nike Community Impact Fund grants
- Eligible for NFL Foundation youth sports grants
- Eligible for Women's Sports Foundation and Title IX-aligned grants
- School districts prefer nonprofit partners (simpler MOU process)
- Corporate sponsors get charitable donation classification (better for their tax treatment)
- More credible story for mission-driven brand partners like Nike

**Action:** Engage a nonprofit/sports law attorney. File IRS Form 1023 (or 1023-EZ for organizations expecting under $50K/year initially). AZ state registration required separately.

**Timeline to 501(c)(3) approval:** 3–6 months for 1023-EZ; 6–12 months for full 1023.
**Workaround:** Use a fiscal sponsor (existing 501(c)(3) sports org) as umbrella while your application is pending. This allows you to accept tax-deductible grants immediately.

## 2. Program Design

### Age Groups
Organize into three competitive tiers:
- **Mini (Ages 8–10):** Focus on fundamentals, fun, inclusion
- **Junior (Ages 11–13):** Skill development, team dynamics
- **Senior (Ages 14–18):** Competitive play, college exposure, showcase events

### Season Structure
- **Season 1 (Inaugural):** 8-week practice program + 4-game mini season
- **Season 2 onward:** Full 12-week season + playoffs + championship event
- **Off-season:** Camps, clinics, skill-development sessions (year-round engagement)

### Coaching Model
- Head coach: Full-time or high part-time commitment, paid competitively
- Assistant coaches: Part-time, paid hourly or per-session
- All coaches: USA Flag Football certified, background checked, SafeSport certified
- Prioritize: Female coaches at all levels (brand alignment + role model value)
- Coach development pipeline: Partner with local universities (ASU, U of A, GCU) for student coaching apprenticeships

### Player Experience Design
The experience that makes players come back AND tell their friends:
- Every player gets a team jersey (Nike-branded if deal is in place)
- Every player gets a highlight video clip posted at least once per season
- Post-game acknowledgment rituals (not just wins — effort, growth, leadership)
- End-of-season awards: Most Improved, Team MVP, Leader on the Field
- Parent communication: Weekly email update, app notification for schedule changes

## 3. School District Strategy

School districts are the most scalable player acquisition channel. One MOU with a district can unlock 50–500 potential players via after-school programming.

### Target Districts (Phase 2)
1. **Phoenix Union High School District** — largest in AZ, Title IX-conscious, serves diverse population
2. **Scottsdale Unified School District** — higher income area, strong parent sports involvement, easier to close sponsorship ties
3. **Tempe Union High School District** — strong athletic culture, multiple high schools with existing football programs

### MOU Terms to Offer
- Free program for first season (builds district trust)
- Organization carries all insurance
- Coaches are certified and background-checked
- Organization provides all equipment
- District provides: field/gym access, school-day announcement access, after-school coordinator liaison

### The Title IX Angle
Title IX requires schools to offer substantially equal athletic opportunities for girls and boys. Many AZ school districts are not yet in compliance for football. Positioning as "we help you fulfill your Title IX obligation at zero cost to the district" is a powerful door-opener.

## 4. Content & Media Strategy

## 7. College Pipeline Program

### Why This Matters for Nike

Flag football is being added at the college level. The NAIA sanctioned women's flag football in 2023. The NCAA is watching. Within 3–5 years, there will be college scholarships for women's flag football.

**Build the pipeline now:**
- Partner with 1–2 college athletic departments (ASU, Grand Canyon University) to create a pathway from your program to college tryouts
- Track any player who advances to college level — these stories are gold for Nike pitch
- Brand positioning: "We created the first girls flag football to college pipeline in AZ"
- Annual "College Showcase Day" event: invite college coaches to watch your Senior division

This differentiates you completely from recreational leagues and makes the Nike national story dramatically stronger.

**Compensation:** Equity/future stake in the organization (if for-profit), or simply mission alignment (if nonprofit). Advisors at this stage typically serve for mission, not money. Offer board title, mentions in press, and access to the journey.

## 9. Insurance Requirements

Before any player touches a field, all of the following must be in place:

| Coverage | Type | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| General Liability | $1M–$2M per occurrence | Protects org from third-party injury claims |
| Participant Accident | Per-player policy | Covers player injuries (medical costs) |
| Directors & Officers | $1M | Protects board from decisions-based claims |
| Coaches Liability | Included in GL or separate | Covers coaching decisions/actions |
| Abuse & Molestation | Separate endorsement | Required by most school districts |

**Provider options:**
- K&K Insurance (youth sports specialist)
- Markel (youth sports policies)
- Via USA Flag Football membership (check included coverage limits)

**Annual cost estimate:** $3,000–$6,000 depending on player count and coverage limits.
